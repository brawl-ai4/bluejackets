All-Time Greats
FRANCHISE
LEGENDS
61
Right Wing · #61 Retired
Rick Nash
The greatest Blue Jacket of all time. All-time leader in goals (289), points (547) and games played (674). Won the Rocket Richard Trophy in 2003–04. Five All-Star selections. Captain 2008–12. Jersey retired 2022.
72
Goaltender · Vezina Winner
Sergei Bobrovsky
Won two Vezina Trophies (2012–13, 2016–17) as the NHL's best goaltender. Led CBJ to four playoff appearances. Career CBJ wins leader (213). Signed a 7-year/$70M deal with Florida in 2019.
13
Right Wing · All-Star
Cam Atkinson
All-time goals leader among American-born CBJ players (213G). Franchise record 6 hat tricks. Two All-Star appearances (2017, 2019). 627 games in Columbus. His #13 honored Oct. 2025.
9
Left Wing · Breadman
Artemi Panarin
The 'Breadman' set single-season franchise records in assists and points (87 pts in 2018–19). Part of the historic 2019 sweep of Tampa Bay. 169 total points in just two seasons. Two-time All-Star as a Jacket.
71
Left Wing · Captain
Nick Foligno
The heart and soul of Columbus for eight seasons. Captain from 2015–21. 142 goals and 334 points in 599 CBJ games. Son of NHL veteran Mike Foligno. Still beloved by the city.
8
Defense · Norris Runner-Up
Zach Werenski
Active franchise all-time assists leader (271+). Finished runner-up for the Norris Trophy after a 23G-59A-82pt season in 2024–25. Alt. Captain and the heart of the CBJ blue line. Signed through 2028.
Through The Years
FRANCHISE
HISTORY
2000
Franchise Founded
The Columbus Blue Jackets are officially founded June 25, 1997, and play their first game October 7, 2000. Columbus becomes the 28th NHL franchise and the cannon fires for the very first time at Nationwide Arena.
2002
Rick Nash — 1st Overall
The Blue Jackets draft Rick Nash 1st overall from the London Knights. He becomes the franchise's greatest-ever player, winning the Rocket Richard Trophy in 2003–04 alongside Kovalchuk and Iginla with 41 goals.
2009
First Playoff Appearance
After nine seasons, the Blue Jackets finally crack the playoffs. Led by captain Rick Nash (40G-39A-79pts) and rookie goalie Steve Mason, Columbus clinches the 7th seed in the West and faces Detroit.
2017
Sergei Bobrovsky's Second Vezina
"Bob" wins his second Vezina Trophy as the NHL's best goaltender, cementing his legacy as the greatest goalie in CBJ history. The Blue Jackets earn a playoff berth, their third consecutive postseason.
2019
The Miracle Sweep
In the greatest upset in NHL history, the Blue Jackets sweep the Tampa Bay Lightning — Presidents' Trophy winners with 62 wins — in the first round. Columbus shocks the hockey world. The city roars for days.
2022
Rick Nash's #61 Retired
The Blue Jackets officially retire Rick Nash's number 61, the first jersey number retired in franchise history. Nash's 289 goals, 547 points, and 674 games remain unreachable franchise records.
2024
Tragedy: The Gaudreau Brothers
The hockey world is devastated when Johnny Gaudreau and his brother Matthew are killed by a drunk driver on August 29, 2024. The 2024–25 season is dedicated to their memory. Columbus grieves and unites.
2026
Building Toward Tomorrow
The 26th season ends with a 40-30-12 record — progress. Adam Fantilli, Kent Johnson, and Jet Greaves are rising stars. Zach Werenski is an elite defender. Nationwide Arena waits for its next chapter.

The Mission
ONE GOAL. ONE CITY. ALL JACKETS.

Columbus is more than a city — it's a state of mind. The Blue Jackets represent the grit, the determination, and the Midwestern heart. From Rick Nash's first goal in 2002 to the miracle sweep in 2019, to the tears for Johnny Gaudreau in 2024, this franchise is built on more than hockey.
