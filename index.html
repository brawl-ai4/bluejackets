Home Fortress
NATIONWIDE

Home to the Blue Jackets since 2000, Nationwide Arena stands as one of the most electric buildings in all of hockey. Capacity: 19,500. Noise level: deafening. The cannon fires every time the Jackets light the lamp — and Columbus goes absolutely wild.

Through The Years
FRANCHISE
HISTORY
2000
Franchise Founded
The Columbus Blue Jackets are officially founded as an NHL expansion team, becoming the 28th franchise in league history. Columbus gets its first major pro sports team.
2000
First Game Ever
The Blue Jackets play their inaugural game on October 7, 2000, facing the Chicago Blackhawks. The cannon fires for the first time at Nationwide Arena.
2003
Rick Nash Arrives
The Blue Jackets draft Rick Nash 1st overall. The Brampton, Ontario native would become the face of the franchise, setting franchise records in goals and becoming a perennial All-Star.
2009
First Playoff Appearance
After nine seasons, the Blue Jackets finally crack the Stanley Cup Playoffs for the very first time in franchise history. The city of Columbus erupts with joy.
2014
Cam Atkinson Era
Cam Atkinson establishes himself as one of the premier power forwards in the Metro Division, becoming the all-time leading goal scorer in Blue Jackets history.
2019
The Miracle Sweep
In one of the biggest upsets in NHL history, the Blue Jackets sweep the Tampa Bay Lightning — the Presidents' Trophy winners with 62 wins — in the first round of the playoffs. Columbus shocks the world.
2020
20th Anniversary
The Blue Jackets celebrate 20 years of hockey in Columbus. A franchise built on toughness, community, and an undying love for the game marks two decades of Union Blue hockey.
2026
The Future Is Now
With a young, hungry roster, the Columbus Blue Jackets look to the future. Nationwide Arena waits for the next chapter — and Columbus is ready to roar again. The cannon stands loaded.
